Requirements

In order to use your SPI-Scan Surveyor unit you will need the imager, a support frame (custom built or supplied by us), tether, pulley (sheave/block with radius >80 mm), junction box, computer (included with some units), a stable - relatively noise-free 24 V DC power source (batteries provided with some models), and a hauling line suitable for the environment and loading. Assembly requires a pair of adjustable or hex spanners (6, 8, and 11 mm). Full disassembly (not recommended in the field) can be accomplished with a set of metric allen-key wrenches (a full set including 2, 2.5, 3, 4, 5, 5.5, 6, and 8 mm), pliers, a circlip pliers, and another adjustable spanner. A flexible soft-rubber (strap-style) pipe wrench is useful for calibration or acrylic tube replacement (not required on new units).

Tether Care and Management

The tether is the single most expensive component of your system. With proper care, your tether will perform will for many years, but carelessness can stop your fieldwork in a hurry.

Bend Radius

Your tether has 12, stranded conductors, strength members, packing material, and several insulating jackets of different sizes. If forcibly bent over any radius smaller than 80 mm, the conductors may part and connectivity may be lost with the scanner permanently. This situation is likely to occur if the scanner is retrieved via a tether bent over the gunwahle, if a smaller block is used to haul a tether under load, or if a loop of cable is trod upon the deck.

First Use

The Kellem's grip has not been properly fixed to your tether. The tether has a small amount of stretching and twisting in it as a result of the manufacturing process. Before attaching the tether to your scanner for the first time, lay it out straight along the ground or in loose loops several metres in diameter. Attach the grip to the assembled instrument and frame as shown in Figure 4. Keeping the imager locked in the up position within the frame, 'walk' the grip up or down the cable until, in its tensioned state, you notice that the electrical plug portion has an appropriate amount of slack.
